About This Visualization

Simple and Powerful tips

Narrative visualization method

July 30, 2017

This project was created based on the hybrid interactive slideshow ordering technique. Guidance is provided on each slide, but the users are free to drill down and list some details or investigate some tangent that they might be interested on or they can choose just to continue.

Scenes

July 30, 2017

This project was created as slides thanks to the awesome vega light library, github and html in order to keep coherence and make it user-friendly. Each scene is a single chart with a specific goal related to the same subject and in a specific order to best convey the message. A double column layout is used in all the slides alternating the position of the text, this guarantees visual consistency. The same font is used throughout the project as well.

Annotations

July 30, 2017

All scene are of this narrative visualization built with annotations, parameters, and triggers. As you roll over the mouse on any of the bar chart provides annotation in the form of x-axis and y axis labels. The titles and short description guides reader to draw conclusion. The best example is bad car sales in USA does not mean the car sales are bad across the world. The car company should start targeting 2nd tier countries as well as there is huge upcoming market and that will help car companies to survive during bad time.

Parameters

July 30, 2017

The graphs are parameterized to show year over year trend for tier 1 and tier 2 countries at the same time the graphs are parameterized for given particular year, it shows the trend the county car sales had from 2005 to 2016.

Triggers

July 30, 2017

The triggers are used to highlight selection in red and gray out the remianing years in given bar graphs. To experience the trigger author will have to select any given year and rest all year are grayed out and graph only highlight year for each country in RED color. The most interesting part is data used in all graphs are same and depending on needs the vega code has been written to satisfy scenes, annotations, parameters, and triggers. .
